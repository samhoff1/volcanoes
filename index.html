Significant Volcano Eruptions

This site lists all significant volcanic eruptions around the world since roughly 4,000 BCE. It was created using Tableau Public, with data from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, for Geography 167.

Volcanoes by explosiveness

The data shows that major volcanic eruptions have been taking place for millenia, and have been recorded in human history since the dawn of time. Of particular interest in the map is the so-called "Rim of Fire" around the Pacific Ocean, where a significant number of volcanic eruptions take place. Other common locations for eruptions include Iceland, which is a geological hotspot.

Worth noting in the data set is that most data points come from the past few hundred years, when human recording became far more reliable and common, and volcanic eruptions could be more accurately measured. Prior to roughly 1,000 AD, there are few records of eruptions that registered below a 6 or 7 on the Volcano Explosivity Index, but in recent times the geologic record is far more thorough and includes almost all measurable eruptions.
